# Grade 4 Mathematics: Advanced Arithmetic and Angles
*Context: Mathematics, Grade 4, Arithmetic, Decimals, Geometry*

## 1. Multi-Digit Multiplication and Division
- Multi-digit multiplication involves multiplying large numbers (e.g., 24 x 15).
- Long division is used to divide large numbers into equal groups.
- A 'Remainder' is the amount left over after division.
- Factors are numbers you multiply together to get another number.
- Multiples are what you get when you multiply a number by others.

## 2. Fractions and Decimals
- 'Equivalent Fractions' are different fractions that name the same amount (e.g., 1/2 and 2/4).
- Decimals are another way to write fractions with denominators of 10 or 100.
- The 'Decimal Point' (.) separates the whole number from the fraction part.
- We can compare and order decimals (e.g., 0.5 is more than 0.25).

## 3. Geometry and Angles
- An 'Angle' is formed when two lines meet at a point.
- Angles are measured in 'Degrees' (°).
- A 'Right Angle' is exactly 90 degrees.
- An 'Acute Angle' is less than 90 degrees.
- An 'Obtuse Angle' is more than 90 degrees.
- Symmetry means a shape looks the same on both sides of a line.

## 4. Units of Measurement
- We can convert units (e.g., 1 foot = 12 inches, 1 pound = 16 ounces).
- Time can be calculated across different intervals.
- Perimeter and Area formulas help solve complex geometric problems.
