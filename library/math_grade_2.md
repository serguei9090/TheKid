# Grade 2 Mathematics: Expanding Numbers
*Context: Mathematics, Grade 2, Arithmetic, Measurement, Time*

## 1. Place Value and Number Sense
- Place value tells us the value of a digit based on its position.
- 'Hundreds', 'Tens', and 'Ones' are the first three places.
- In the number 123, there is 1 hundred, 2 tens, and 3 ones.
- Numbers can be 'Even' or 'Odd'.
- Even numbers end in 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8.
- Odd numbers end in 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.

## 2. Advanced Addition and Subtraction
- We can add and subtract numbers up to 100.
- 'Regrouping' is used when a column adds up to 10 or more.
- Subtracting 2-digit numbers sometimes requires 'borrowing' from the tens place.
- Mentally adding 10 or 100 to a number change only the digit in that place.

## 3. Measurement and Money
- Length can be measured in 'Inches' or 'Centimeters'.
- 'Weight' tells us how heavy something is.
- 'Money' has different values: Penny (1 cent), Nickel (5 cents), Dime (10 cents), Quarter (25 cents).
- One hundred cents make one 'Dollar' ($).

## 4. Time and Data
- A clock shows 'Hours' and 'Minutes'.
- There are 60 minutes in one hour.
- There are 24 hours in one day.
- A 'Bar Graph' uses bars to show information.
- A 'Tally Chart' uses marks to count things.
