# Sentence Mechanics and Punctuation (Grade 1)
*Context: Grammar, Punctuation, Writing*

## 1. Starting and Ending
- Every sentence must begin with a capital letter.
- A capital letter is a big letter.
- A statement ends with a period (.).
- A question ends with a question mark (?).
- An exclamation or strong feeling ends with an exclamation mark (!).

## 2. Punctuation Rules
- A Period (.) shows that the sentence is finished.
- A Question Mark (?) shows that someone is asking for an answer.
- An Exclamation Mark (!) shows that someone is excited or loud.
- A Comma (,) is a short pause in a sentence.
- A Comma (,) is used to separate items in a list.

## 3. Names and Capitals
- The name of a person always starts with a capital letter.
- The name of a place always starts with a capital letter.
- The word 'I' is always capitalized when used as a pronoun.
- Days of the week and months of the year start with capital letters.

## 4. Proper Sentence Examples
- CORRECT: 'The cat is on the mat.' (Starts with capital, ends with period)
- CORRECT: 'Where is the ball?' (Starts with capital, ends with question mark)
- CORRECT: 'I love to play!' (Starts with capital, uses capitalized I, ends with exclamation)
- INCORRECT: 'the cat is on the mat' (No capital, no period)
