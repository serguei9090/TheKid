# Algebra Fundamentals & Mathematical Logic
*Context: Mathematics, Algebra, Equations, Variables, Logic*

## 1. Variables and Unknowns
- A 'Variable' is a symbol, typically a letter like 'x' or 'y', used to represent an unknown number.
- In algebra, variables allow us to write mathematical rules and equations that apply generally.
- An 'Expression' is a mathematical phrase containing numbers, operations, and variables (e.g., '3x + 5').
- An expression does not contain an equals sign.

## 2. Equations and Equality
- An 'Equation' is a mathematical statement asserting that two expressions are equal.
- The 'Equals Sign' (=) represents exact mathematical equality.
- To 'Solve an Equation' means to find the value of the variable that makes the equation true.
- If an equation states 'x + 5 = 10', then the variable 'x' must equal '5'.
- Whatever mathematical operation is done to one side of an equation must also be performed on the other side to maintain equality.

## 3. The Order of Operations (PEMDAS)
- Mathematical operations must be performed in a specific, strict order.
- 'Parentheses' (P) operations are always calculated first.
- 'Exponents' (E) operations are calculated second.
- 'Multiplication' (M) and 'Division' (D) are calculated third, strictly from left to right.
- 'Addition' (A) and 'Subtraction' (S) are calculated last, strictly from left to right.
- For example, in '4 + 3 * 2', the multiplication '3 * 2' equals '6', resulting in '4 + 6', which equals '10'. It does not equal '14'.

## 4. Fundamental Properties of Arithmetic
- The 'Commutative Property of Addition' states that the order of numbers does not change their sum (e.g., 'a + b = b + a').
- The 'Commutative Property of Multiplication' states that the order of numbers does not change their product (e.g., 'a * b = b * a').
- Subtraction and Division are not commutative operations.
- The 'Distributive Property' allows us to multiply a term across terms inside parentheses (e.g., 'a(b + c) = ab + ac').

## 5. Absolute Truth in Mathematics
- Mathematical facts are unchangeable and constant.
- If 'a = b' and 'b = c', then the Transitive Property dictates that 'a = c'.
- The sum of two positive integers is always a larger positive integer.
- Multiplying a number by zero always results in a product of zero.
- Dividing any real number by zero is mathematically undefined.
