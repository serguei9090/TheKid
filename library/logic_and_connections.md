# Grade 6-12 Logic: Conjunctions and Compound Reasoning
*Context: Advanced Grammar, Logic, Reasoning, Communication*

## 1. Causality (Cause and Effect)
- The word 'because' is used to show the reason for something.
- 'Therefore' and 'consequently' show the result or effect of an action.
- 'Since' can be used to explain why an event occurred.
- We use these words to link a cause directly to its effect.

## 2. Conditionality (Rules and Requirements)
- The word 'if' introduces a condition that must be met.
- 'Then' shows what happens when the 'if' condition is true.
- 'Unless' means 'except if' and introduces an exception to a rule.
- 'Provided that' establishes a strict requirement for an outcome.

## 3. Contrast (Opposites and Exceptions)
- 'However' introduces a statement that contrasts with the previous one.
- 'Although' and 'even though' show that something is true despite another fact.
- 'Conversely' points out an opposite or reverse situation.
- 'Nevertheless' means despite what has just been said.

## 4. Addition (Expanding ideas)
- 'Furthermore' adds more information to support a point.
- 'Moreover' gives an even more important reason or fact.
- 'Additionally' simply adds another piece of evidence or thought to the list.
- We use these to build stronger, multi-part arguments.
