# Grade 1 Mathematics: The Foundation
*Context: Mathematics, Grade 1, Arithmetic, Geometry*

## 1. Numbers and Counting
- Numbers follow a specific sequence: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5...
- We can count up to 120 and beyond.
- 'Zero' means nothing or none.
- 'One' is more than 'Zero'.
- Ten 'Ones' make one 'Ten'.
- Grouping numbers into tens and ones makes counting easier.

## 2. Basic Addition and Subtraction
- Addition means putting things together.
- The symbol for plus is '+'.
- Subtraction means taking things away.
- The symbol for minus is '-'.
- The symbol for equals is '='.
- 1 + 1 = 2 (One plus one equals two).
- 2 - 1 = 1 (Two minus one equals one).
- Adding 'Zero' to a number does not change it.
- Subtracting a number from itself equals 'Zero'.

## 3. Shapes and Geometry
- A 'Circle' is round with no corners.
- A 'Square' has four equal sides and four corners.
- A 'Triangle' has three sides and three corners.
- A 'Rectangle' has four sides and four corners, but not all sides are equal.
- Shapes are everywhere in our world.

## 4. Measuring and Logic
- 'More' is a larger amount.
- 'Less' is a smaller amount.
- We can compare lengths: some things are 'Longer' and some are 'Shorter'.
- A 'Pattern' is a sequence that repeats (e.g., Circle, Square, Circle, Square).
